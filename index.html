Reading Accessibility for Visually Impaired
Towards more Inclusive STEM Education in India

About Project

Reading Accessibility for Visually Impaired (RAVI) project is supported by Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD), Govt. of India (GoI). The main objective of the project is to make Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM) eBooks accessible for persons with visual impairment.

Increasingly the text accessed by sighted users is becoming digital. Interestingly for the visually impaired also, the digital text is easily accessible using screen-reading software like NVDA, Google TalkBack, etc. However, visually impaired face significant challenges if they need to access text mixed with mathematical equations and figures, and text in regional languages.

A vast variety of mathematical content is available in the form of non-standard encoding and images. Tools and techniques are required to convert them into standard formats like MathML (Mathematics Mark-up Language). Even mathematical content in standard format has complexity in audio rendering due to its two-dimensional nature and contextual dependency. Another gap appears in making text-book diagrams accessible. Diagrams in STEM text-books convey vital information which is sometimes not available in the textual content. Alt-text generation/augmentation becomes more challenging when it comes to handling verbosity on the basis of context. Image description APIs are currently far behind in providing adequate description of text-book diagrams. Tools and techniques are required for automatic alt-text generation/augmention. Further, textual content available in regional languages adds another layer of inaccessibility due to use of non-unicode encodings. Issues related to access of Indian language material using screen readers will also be addressed in this project. Therefore, the project aims to address the following issues:

  • Navigating and accessing complex equations in the text in a comprehensible manner
  • Exploring automatic/semi-automatic generation of text that needs to be augmented for describing a diagram to the visually impaired
  • Issues related to access of Indian language material using screen readers on platforms like Android
